# GST Compliance Checklist for MSMEs

## Registration Requirements

### When is GST Registration Mandatory?
□ Annual turnover exceeds ₹40 lakhs (₹20 lakhs for special states)
□ Inter-state supply of goods/services
□ Online selling through e-commerce platforms
□ Casual taxable person
□ Non-resident taxable person

### Optional Registration
□ Annual turnover between ₹20-40 lakhs (can opt for voluntary registration)
□ Composition scheme eligibility (turnover up to ₹1.5 crores)

## Required Documents for GST Registration

□ PAN Card
□ Aadhaar Card
□ Proof of business registration/incorporation
□ Proof of business address
□ Bank account statement/cancelled cheque
□ Authorization letter (if filed by CA/representative)
□ Digital signature or e-sign facility

## Monthly Compliance Requirements

### For Regular Taxpayers
□ File GSTR-1 (Sales return) by 11th of next month
□ File GSTR-3B (Summary return) by 20th of next month
□ Pay GST liability by 20th of next month
□ Reconcile input tax credit

### For Composition Dealers
□ File GSTR-4 (Quarterly return)
□ Pay GST quarterly
□ Maintain simplified records

## Annual Compliance

□ File GSTR-9 (Annual return) by December 31st
□ File GSTR-9C (Audit report) if turnover > ₹2 crores
□ Conduct annual audit if required

## Record Keeping Requirements

### Tax Invoices
□ Maintain proper tax invoices for all sales
□ Include all mandatory fields in invoices
□ Sequential numbering of invoices
□ Digital signatures where applicable

### Input Documents
□ Maintain all purchase invoices
□ Keep delivery challans
□ Store import documents
□ Keep expense receipts

### Books of Accounts
□ Maintain books of accounts as per GST rules
□ Keep records for 72 months from due date of annual return
□ Digital backup of all records

## Input Tax Credit (ITC) Compliance

□ Claim ITC only on eligible supplies
□ Ensure supplier has paid GST to government
□ Reconcile ITC with supplier's outward supplies
□ Reverse ITC on supplies used for non-business purposes

## Common Compliance Pitfalls to Avoid

□ Late filing of returns (attracts penalty)
□ Incorrect invoice formats
□ Claiming ineligible ITC
□ Not paying GST on time
□ Poor record maintenance
□ Not reconciling ITC regularly

## E-way Bill Requirements

□ Generate e-way bill for goods transport > ₹50,000
□ Update Part-B for goods transport
□ Ensure validity period compliance
□ Cancel e-way bill if goods not transported

## Special Provisions for MSMEs

□ Quarterly return filing option (if turnover < ₹5 crores)
□ Composition scheme benefits
□ Reduced compliance burden
□ Extended time limits for certain provisions

## Penalty Structure (to be aware of)

- Late return filing: ₹200 per day (₹100 per return for NIL returns)
- Late payment of tax: 18% per annum interest
- Not maintaining proper records: ₹25,000
- Not issuing proper invoices: ₹10,000

## Important Dates to Remember

### Monthly (for regular taxpayers)
- 10th: Last date for auto-drafting GSTR-2B
- 11th: GSTR-1 filing due date
- 20th: GSTR-3B filing and payment due date

### Quarterly (for composition dealers)
- 18th of month following quarter end: GSTR-4 due date

### Annual
- December 31st: GSTR-9 due date
- January 31st: GSTR-9C due date (if applicable)
